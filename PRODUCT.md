# Product

## Register

product

## Users

Small Malaysian law firm, 2-5 people. Primary users are a 40s lawyer and their admin staff — not tech-savvy, first-time app users. They handle tenancy agreements daily, currently on paper and Word docs. They need something that feels immediately familiar, not intimidating, and gets the job done without a learning curve.

Secondary user: the lawyer's clients (landlords and tenants) who receive WhatsApp notifications and stamped PDFs. They never touch the app directly.

## Product Purpose

Sewasah Agree automates the tenancy agreement workflow for Malaysian law firms — from collecting client details to generating a legally sound agreement, getting lawyer approval, submitting for LHDN stamping via eDutiSetem, and delivering the stamped document to all parties. What used to take 2-3 hours now takes 10 minutes.

Success looks like: a 40-year-old lawyer's admin can create and deliver a stamped tenancy agreement without calling the lawyer or opening Word.

## Brand Personality

Warm, trustworthy, effortless. Like a knowledgeable friend who handles the paperwork for you — not a cold legal system, not a flashy SaaS tool.

## Anti-references

- Government portals (MyTax, LHDN) — grey, confusing, intimidating
- Corporate law firm websites — dark navy, formal, cold
- Generic SaaS dashboards — identical card grids, gradient text, hollow metrics
- Overly playful fintech (too many emojis, too bright, feels unserious for legal documents)

## Design Principles

1. **Clarity over cleverness.** Every screen has one primary action. No cognitive load from options competing for attention.
2. **Warmth builds trust.** Legal tools feel cold by default. Warm neutrals and human copy make the stakes feel manageable.
3. **Progress is visible.** Users always know where they are in the workflow. No dead ends, no confusion about what happens next.
4. **Earn the animation.** Motion is purposeful — it confirms actions, guides attention, never decorates.
5. **Malay-English context.** Copy can mix naturally. Nothing stiff or over-translated.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

- WCAG AA minimum
- Large enough touch targets for mobile (lawyer reviews on phone)
- High contrast text on warm backgrounds
- No motion that cannot be reduced
